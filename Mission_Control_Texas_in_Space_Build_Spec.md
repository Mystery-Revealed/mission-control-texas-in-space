# "Mission Control: Texas in Space" — Build Specification
### Unit 7 Game · Contemporary Texas · *Common Build Standards apply (Pattern A — Engine Game)*

## 1. At a Glance
| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| **TEKS** | 7.12C (aerospace industry's impact), 7.19A/C/E (technology's effects; interdependence), 7.7E (contemporary era) |
| **Role** | A young flight controller at NASA's Johnson Space Center, Houston (everyone plays the controller — class-wide group) |
| **Type** | Solo problem-solving game — one mission in 6 phases × 2 decisions = **12 graded actions**; a console-style status strip (GO/NO-GO lights) |
| **Adapter** | `missionControl.js` · meters `{ mission, crewSafety, team }` start 50 |

**Pitch:** "Houston" was the first word spoken from the Moon — and tonight, Houston is you. Take your console for a full mission, work the problems the way flight controllers really do, and find out why the road to space runs through Texas.

**Teaching idea:** JSC (opened 1961–63) made Houston "Space City" and anchored the aerospace industry your outline names as modern Texas's pride. The game teaches the **controller's method** — checklists, call-and-response, "work the problem," crew safety above schedule — as living proof of 7.19's technology-and-society standard. Decisions are procedurally *right* the way real mission rules are: the calm, checklist answer wins.

## 2. Content Bank
**Johnson Space Center:** NASA's home for astronaut training and **Mission Control** since Gemini (1965); "Houston, Tranquility Base here… the Eagle has landed" (1969) · the flight-controller culture: **flight director's rules**, GO/NO-GO polls around the room, every console an expert voice, **"tough and competent"** (the post-Apollo 1 creed) · **Apollo 13 (1970):** "Houston, we've had a problem" — the master class in working the problem (power-down, the CO₂ scrubber improvisation, the whole room inventing survival) · shuttle and ISS eras: Houston runs the station's American side around the clock · the industry around it: thousands of Texas engineering jobs, contractors, and today's commercial-space boom (Houston to Boca Chica) — aerospace as economic diversification (7.12C, the outline's "beyond oil" theme) · spinoff tech and medical partnership with Houston's hospitals (7.19E interdependence).

## 3. Mechanics
Meters: **Mission** 🚀 (objectives on track), **Crew Safety** ❤️ (always outranks Mission — the design says so), **Team** 🎧 (the room's trust in your calls). Ending = sum → tiers ("Flight, We're GO" / "Splashdown" / "Long Night in the Trench") + debrief: JSC's story, the 18-wheeler of jobs aerospace pulls through Texas, and the method — *calm, checklist, crew first* — as the thing Texas gave spaceflight.

## 4. Sample Scripted Phase + Beat Matrix
**Phase 1 — Console Prep (decision 1):** *Launch is tomorrow. Your console's telemetry looked odd for two seconds in the last simulation.*
- ✅ **Flag it, pull the data, and run the checklist with your back-room team tonight.** Team +10, Mission +5. *"Controllers chase every anomaly to ground. 'It was probably nothing' isn't in the vocabulary."*
- ⚠️ **Watch for it again during launch.** Mission +5, Team −5. *"Watching is not the same as knowing."*
- ❌ **Two seconds? Let it go.** Crew Safety −10. *"The two-second blips are how big days go wrong."*

**Phase 1 (decision 2 — the GO/NO-GO poll):** *Your data is clean but a gut feeling nags.* ✅ Call "GO" on the data — gut feelings get logged, data gets voted (Team +10; *"the poll runs on evidence"*) · ⚠️ Call "NO-GO" on the feeling (Team −5; honest feedback: the room respects caution but asks for the data) · ❌ Stay silent in the poll (Team −10; *"a silent console is a hole in the wall"*).

**Beat matrix:**
| Phase | Beat | Right answer teaches |
|---|---|---|
| 2 | Launch | Calls in sequence, eyes on your own console, trust the room (✅ division of expertise) |
| 3 | The anomaly (Apollo-13-inspired) | A tank pressure drops: **work the problem** — power priorities, safe modes, no guessing aloud (✅ the famous method); Crew Safety explicitly outranks the mission timeline (✅) |
| 4 | The workaround | The scrubber-style improvisation: inventory what's *actually aboard* and build the fix from it (✅ constraint creativity — the game's best beat) |
| 5 | The long watch | Rotate rest, brief your relief precisely, log everything (✅ endurance + handoff discipline — how the ISS is really flown from Houston) |
| 6 | Reentry & splashdown | Blackout patience (✅ trust the physics and the prep); then the debrief culture — what we fix before next time (✅ "tough and competent" closer) |
